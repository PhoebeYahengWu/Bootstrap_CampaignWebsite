About Abdourahamane Diallo

Abdourahamane Diallo, 28, was born and raised in Guinea. After studying law at La Source University, Diallo moved to the United States to further his education.

Like many immigrants from non-English speaking countries, he faced the challenges of navigating a new environment, a new language and a new culture. To make ends meet, he worked as a taxi driver, dishwasher and delivery worker among other jobs.

Amid these challenges, he enrolled at John Jay College of Criminal Justice of the City University of New York where he studied political science for two years. During his courses, Diallo developed a curiosity for understanding the role of financial literacy and economic policies in community development. He transferred to Baruch College where he earned a Bachelor of Business Administration, majoring in finance and investment and minoring in communication studies with a focus on conflict resolution. He later went on to earn a Certificate of Leadership Foundation from Columbia University.

At Baruch, Diallo mobilized his colleagues and founded the Guinean Students Association (GSA), serving as president for two years. His initiative has since grown and currently reaches more than 10,000 people. There are also GSA chapters at the New York City College of Technology, the City College of New York and the Borough of Manhattan Community College. GSA has helped hundreds of students and young professionals secure internships and full-time jobs by hosting professional development workshops, sponsoring students to attend professional conferences and organizing an annual networking conference. As of today, the Guinean Students Association; Guinean Graduates, a platform that highlights and honors graduates of Guinean descent; and EachOne LiftOne, a mentorship program, are under an umbrella organization called Guineans Succeeding in America (GSA).

Outside of the Guinean community, Diallo advocates for the Bronx community as the general secretary for the Bronx Borough President’s African Advisory Council and a member of Bronx Community Board 3.

Diallo was recently accepted to the Schwarzman Scholars Program, one of the world’s most prestigious graduate fellowships. He is the first Baruch College student to be admitted to the program. He will be pursuing a master’s degree in global affairs with a focus on economic power and diplomacy at Tsinghua University.
